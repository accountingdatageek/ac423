Illustrative Examples
  • Missing approval on one low-dollar expense
  • Password policy exception on non-financial system
  • Minor reconciliation delay on small account
  • AP clerk can edit vendor master file; partial compensating review
  • ~20% of inventory counts reconciled late; no adjustments found
  • Weak segregation of duties in payroll; supervisor review exists
  • CFO can post manual JEs to revenue with no review
  • ERP allows unrestricted modification by IT
  • Auditor finds $6M error management's controls missed

Key Insight — Aggregation: Multiple control deficiencies that individually fall below the SD or MW threshold must be evaluated in combination. Deficiencies affecting related processes, accounts, or assertions can aggregate to a higher severity classification under PCAOB AS 2201.65–.67. Auditors must consider whether the combined exposure creates a reasonable possibility of material misstatement.

Severity Assessment Factors (AS 2201.65–.68)

Exposure & Nature Factors

  • Financial statement accounts and assertions affected
  • Susceptibility to loss or fraud
  • Subjectivity and complexity of the assertion
  • Volume of transactions flowing through the control

Mitigating Factors

  • Existence and operating effectiveness of compensating controls
  • Monitoring controls (internal audit, management review)
  • Nature of the financial reporting element involved
  • Interaction with other controls in the same cycle

AS 2201.69 Strong MW Indicators

  • Restatement of previously issued financial statements
  • Auditor detects material misstatement entity missed
  • Audit committee provides ineffective oversight
  • Material fraud by senior management
  • Ineffective internal audit / risk assessment function
