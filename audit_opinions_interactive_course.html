Modification triggers
Material Misstatement
Financial statements contain amounts or disclosures that are materially misstated. Includes departures from GAAP/IFRS, inadequate disclosures, and inappropriate accounting policies. Leads to Qualified (material, not pervasive) or Adverse (material and pervasive).
Scope Limitation
Auditor unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence for a material area. Can be client-imposed or circumstantial (e.g., lost records, inaccessible inventory). Leads to Qualified (material, not pervasive) or Disclaimer (material and pervasive).

Governing standards framework
AS
PCAOB
Public Company Accounting Oversight Board
  • AS 3101 — The Auditor's Report on an Audit of Financial Statements
  • AS 2101 — Audit Planning
  • AS 2105 — Consideration of Materiality
  • AS 2810 — Evaluating Audit Results
  • Applies to SEC registrants and public company audits in the U.S.
PCAOB requires a two-paragraph structure for the opinion (opinion paragraph + basis for opinion) and disclosure of Critical Audit Matters (CAMs) for large accelerated filers.
ISA
IAASB / ISA
International Standards on Auditing
  • ISA 700 — Forming an Opinion and Reporting
  • ISA 701 — Key Audit Matters (KAMs)
  • ISA 705 — Modifications to the Opinion
  • ISA 706 — Emphasis of Matter / Other Matter Paragraphs
  • Adopted by 130+ countries; basis for most global audits
ISA 705 provides the primary framework distinguishing qualified, adverse, and disclaimer opinions based on the nature and pervasiveness of the issue.
AU-C
AICPA / GAAS
Generally Accepted Auditing Standards
  • AU-C 700 — Forming an Opinion and Reporting
  • AU-C 705 — Modifications to the Opinion
  • AU-C 706 — Emphasis of Matter / Other Matter
  • AU-C 570 — Going Concern
  • Applies to non-public company audits in the U.S.
AICPA standards are largely converged with ISA but maintained separately. Going concern uncertainty triggers an EOM paragraph under AU-C 570.
SEC
SEC Requirements
Securities and Exchange Commission
  • Regulation S-X — Form and content of financial statements
  • Staff Accounting Bulletins — SEC staff interpretations
  • Requires PCAOB standards for all registrants
  • Non-standard opinions may trigger comment letters
  • Going concern opinions can trigger trading halts / default provisions
A modified opinion on a public company filing is an SEC disclosure event and can have significant market consequences beyond the audit itself.
